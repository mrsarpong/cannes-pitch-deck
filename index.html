The Problem
The conversation is happening.
The problem is who is having it.
Africa's presence at Cannes is growing. The creator economy is on the agenda. The conversation about African markets, talent, and soft power has arrived.
Most voices shaping that conversation are Western consultants, diaspora-adjacent executives, and researchers observing Africa from the outside. Very few have spent the last decade building independent media from inside Accra, covering the culture as it happens, and documenting, with evidence, exactly why Africa's creative talent keeps producing world-class output while the systems around it keep failing to convert talent into sustainable economic value.
That is the gap the session fills.
The Thesis · Digital Sharecropping
Africa's most creative talent is functioning as high-performing digital sharecroppers for global tech giants; the brands celebrating African culture are funding the system that keeps it that way.
Documented · Provable · Nearly a decade of journalism from inside Ghana's creative industry
African tech and media journalists are documenting the same underlying economic problem; in February 2026, TechCabal published analysis naming value capture as Africa's defining creative economy challenge. The specific intellectual framework, Digital Sharecropping, is mine. The receipts are in the work. Cannes provides the room where the people responsible for closing the gap are sitting.
